# PerceptionQuiz — Meta Ads Marketing Strategy

**Date:** February 2026
**Budget:** $100-500/month to start, scaling up once winners are found
**Starting point:** Zero audience, no brand accounts

---

## 1. Best Ad Formats for Quiz Products

**Video (4:5 vertical, 15-30 seconds) is the clear winner.** 70-80% of Meta ad performance comes from creative quality, not targeting or budget.

| Format | Use Case | Priority |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Short vertical video (4:5) | Primary format for cold traffic | #1 |
| Story/Reels ads | Essential for 18-35 demographic | #2 |
| Static image ads | Secondary — good for retargeting | #3 |
| Carousel ads | Not ideal for cold traffic on quiz products | Low |

**For PerceptionQuiz specifically:** Video ads that tease the concept ("your friends see you differently than you think") and end with a CTA to take the quiz. Show the share mechanic visually — seeing someone share a link and their friends reacting demonstrates the product in one shot.

## 2. Typical CPA Benchmarks

| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| Average Meta CPA (all industries) | $30 |
| Education/personal development | $15-25 |
| Quiz/lead gen products | $10-20 |
| **Our target CPA** | **$8-15 paid** |

At $7.99 revenue per sale, you need CPA below $8 to be margin-positive on paid alone. **The viral sharing loop is what makes the economics work** — if your K-factor (virality coefficient) is 0.3 (every 10 buyers invite 3 more who convert), your true blended CPA drops to ~77% of your paid CPA.

## 3. Targeting Strategy

**Start broad, then narrow based on data:**

1. **Broad targeting (ages 18-35, US):** Meta's algorithm performs better with broader audiences because it has more data. Start here.
2. **Interest targeting to test:**
   - Psychology, personality tests, self-improvement, mindfulness
   - 16Personalities, Myers-Briggs, Enneagram audiences
   - Relationship content, friendship content
   - BuzzFeed-style entertainment/quiz content
3. **Lookalike audiences:** Only after 100+ purchases or 500+ quiz completions
4. **Retargeting:** Set up immediately. Target site visitors who didn't purchase. Should eventually get 20-30% of budget.

**Avoid:** Hyper-narrow interest stacks at this budget level.

## 4. Testing Framework ($100-500/Month)

### Phase 1: $100-200/month (Month 1)

- 1 campaign, 1 ad set, 3 ad creatives maximum
- All creatives test different **hooks** (opening 3 seconds), same offer/CTA
- Budget: $5-7/day
- Goal: find which hook generates lowest CPM and highest CTR
- Run minimum 7 days before drawing conclusions

### Phase 2: $200-400/month (Month 2)

- Promote winning hook to its own ad set at $8-10/day
- Test 2 new hooks against each other in a second ad set at $3-4/day
- Begin retargeting ad set at $3-5/day
- Total: 3 ad sets, 5-6 creatives

### Phase 3: Scale (Month 3+)

- Double budget on any ad set returning CPA below $15
- Keep testing 1-2 new creatives per month (creative fatigue sets in at 7-14 days)

### Budget Split: Testing vs. Scaling

| Phase | Testing | Scaling | Retargeting |
|-------|---------|---------|-------------|
| Launch ($100-200/mo) | 80% | — | 20% |
| Growth ($300-500/mo) | 50% | 30% | 20% |
| Scale ($500+/mo) | 20% | 60% | 20% |

### Important: Optimization Events

Meta recommends 50 conversions per ad set per week. At $7.99 you won't hit this early on. So:

- **Weeks 1-4:** Optimize for "Quiz Start" (View Content)
- **Month 2:** Shift to mid-funnel event (quiz completion)
- **Month 3+:** Shift to Purchase optimization once you have 100+ pixel purchase events

## 5. Ad Creative Best Practices

### Hooks That Work for Quiz Products

The first 3 seconds determine everything. Top-performing structures:

1. **Identity challenge:** "You think you know yourself. Your friends disagree."
2. **Curiosity gap reveal (UGC):** "I just found out my friends see me completely differently than I see myself."
3. **Social proof tease:** "My friends nailed 11 out of 15 things about me — and missed on the ones that mattered most."
4. **Direct challenge:** "How well do your closest friends actually know you?"
5. **FOMO play:** "Everyone in my friend group is doing this and the results are brutal."
6. **Reverse psychology:** "Don't take this quiz if you're not ready to find out what your friends really think."

### Static Ad Headlines

- "Find out how your friends really see you"
- "Your self-image vs. your friends' perception — the gap might surprise you"
- "Takes 5 minutes. The results last a lifetime."
- "How different are you from who you think you are?"

### UGC vs. Branded Creative

**UGC wins definitively for this category:**

- UGC ads have 4x higher CTR and 50% lower CPC on Meta (2025 data)
- The product is about authentic self-perception — UGC is tonally consistent
- UGC feels like a friend recommendation, which IS the value proposition

**How to source UGC at zero budget:** Give the app to 20 friends for free. Ask them to film their reaction when they see their results vs. friends' responses. These unfiltered reactions are your best creative assets.

### Mystery vs. Social Proof

- **Cold traffic ads:** Lean into mystery/curiosity. Show that a gap exists but not what it is
- **Retargeting ads:** Switch to social proof. Show real results, testimonials, screenshots

## 6. Funnel Structure

```
Meta Ad (video, curiosity hook)
    → Landing page (minimal — headline, 3 bullets, 1 CTA)
        → Free quiz begins (15 questions)
            → Results preview / teaser shown (partial, blurred)
                → Paywall: "See your full report" — $7.99
                    → Share mechanic unlocked post-purchase
                        → Friends receive link, take perspective quiz
                            → Original user sees comparison report
```

### Landing Page

Keep it extremely minimal:
- One headline matching the ad
- One subheadline
- 2-3 bullet points of what they'll discover
- 1 social proof element (star rating or user count)
- Single large CTA button ("Take the Quiz Free")
- No navigation. No footer links. Under 500 words.

### Conversion Rate Benchmarks

| Stage | Benchmark |
|-------|-----------|
| Ad click → quiz start | 30-50% |
| Quiz start → completion | 66-85% |
| Quiz completion → paywall view | 95-99% |
| Paywall view → purchase | 3-8% |
| Purchaser → shares link | Target 25-40% |

**At $7.99, a 5% paywall conversion rate is your baseline to beat.**

### Paywall Strategy

1. After quiz completion, show a "calculating your results" animation (builds anticipation)
2. Reveal 2-3 non-specific teaser insights for free
3. Show blurred/partially visible results card
4. Paywall: position as "unlock the comparison" not "buy a report"
5. Show exactly what they get: full self-assessment + shareable link + side-by-side comparison
6. Add social proof: "4.8 stars from 2,400+ reports"
7. Urgency: "Share within 48 hours to collect responses"

### Pricing Test (Month 3)

Test $4.99 vs. $7.99 vs. $9.99. A lower price may drive more purchasers and shares, increasing viral coefficient. Optimize for revenue-per-100-visitors, not revenue-per-purchase.

## 7. Key Metrics to Track

**Priority order during testing phase:**

| Metric | Target | What It Tells You |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| Hook rate (3s watch) | >15% good, >30% strong | Is your creative concept working? |
| CTR (link clicks) | 1.0-2.5% cold traffic | Are people interested enough to click? |
| CPM | $10-25 ideal | Is Meta rewarding your creative? |
| Quiz start rate | 40-60% of page visitors | Is your landing page working? |
| Quiz completion rate | 65%+ of starters | Are questions too long/boring? |
| Cost per completion | <$3 | Is your funnel efficient? |
| Paywall conversion | 4-8% | Is your offer compelling? |
| Share rate post-purchase | 25-40% | Is your viral loop working? |

### Kill Criteria (After 7+ Days)

- CTR below 0.5% → creative isn't stopping the scroll
- CPM above $60 → Meta penalizing creative quality
- Quiz start rate below 15% of clicks → landing page broken
- CPC above $3.00 → too expensive at this budget

### Scale Criteria

- CTR above 1.5% on cold traffic
- CPC below $1.50
- Quiz completion rate above 60%
- Any purchase CPA below $15

**Budget increases:** 20% increments only. Doubling overnight resets Meta's learning phase. Increase on Monday/Tuesday (lower CPM days).

---

## 8. When to Scale

Scale when ALL of these are true:

- [ ] CPA consistently below $15 over a 14-day window
- [ ] Quiz completion rate above 60%
- [ ] Paywall conversion rate above 4%
- [ ] Share rate above 20%
- [ ] 3+ creatives tested with a clear winner
- [ ] Pixel has 100+ purchase events

**Realistic timeline:** 8-12 weeks at $200-400/month to hit all checkboxes.

**Scaling path:** $200 → $500 → $1,000 → $2,500/month. Each doubling preceded by 2 weeks of stable performance.

## 10. Strategic Priorities

1. **Your viral loop IS your business model.** Every decision should ask "does this increase share rate?" Ad economics only work with viral sharing.

2. **UGC from real users will outperform anything you produce.** Get 20-30 beta users and capture genuine reactions. This footage is your best creative asset.

3. **Start with curiosity-gap hooks, not feature explanations.** Nobody searches for "self-perception quiz." They respond to "your friends see you differently than you think."

4. **Track cost per quiz completion as your north star metric in months 1-2.** You won't have enough purchase data yet.

5. **Set up your Meta Pixel with custom events BEFORE running any ads:**
   - Quiz Start (View Content)
   - Quiz Complete
   - Paywall View (Add to Cart)
   - Purchase
   - Share Link (custom event)
