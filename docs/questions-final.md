# MirrorQuiz — Final Questions (v3)

Based on the best questions from v1 (research-grounded) and v2-alt (conversational, balanced). 12 questions, 6 categories, 2 per category. No reverse scoring. Uniform 5-point agreement scale.

## Scale

1 = Strongly Disagree | 2 = Disagree | 3 = Neutral | 4 = Agree | 5 = Strongly Agree

---

## Questions

### Category 1: Warmth

**Q1 — Including others**
- **Self:** "I go out of my way to include people who seem left out or quiet in a group."
- **Friend:** "[Name] goes out of their way to include people who seem left out or quiet in a group."
- **Expected gap:** Blind spot. Most people overrate this.

**Q2 — Making people feel heard**
- **Self:** "I make people feel heard when they talk to me."
- **Friend:** "[Name] makes you feel heard when you talk to them."
- **Expected gap:** Mixed. High-empathy people underrate (hidden strength). Self-focused people overrate (blind spot).

---

### Category 2: Humor

**Q3 — Making people laugh**
- **Self:** "I regularly make the people around me genuinely laugh."
- **Friend:** "[Name] regularly makes you genuinely laugh."
- **Expected gap:** Mixed (often hidden strength). Funny people often don't realize how funny they are. High shareability.

**Q4 — Lightening the mood**
- **Self:** "When things get tense or awkward, I'm the one who lightens the mood."
- **Friend:** "When things get tense or awkward, [Name] is the one who lightens the mood."
- **Expected gap:** Moderate. Tests tension diffusion — a specific social skill people have inaccurate self-beliefs about.

---

### Category 3: Directness

**Q5 — Telling hard truths**
- **Self:** "If a friend is making a mistake, I tell them directly rather than hinting or staying quiet."
- **Friend:** "If you were making a mistake, [Name] would tell you directly rather than hinting or staying quiet."
- **Expected gap:** Blind spot. Most people believe they're more direct than they actually are.

**Q6 — Taking feedback**
- **Self:** "When someone gives me critical feedback, I take it seriously instead of getting defensive."
- **Friend:** "When [Name] gets critical feedback, they take it seriously instead of getting defensive."
- **Expected gap:** Blind spot. The least receptive people rate themselves the most receptive. The irony is the insight.

---

### Category 4: Reliability

**Q7 — Following through**
- **Self:** "When I say I'll do something — text back, show up, send that thing — I follow through."
- **Friend:** "When [Name] says they'll do something, they actually follow through."
- **Expected gap:** Blind spot. Most universally relatable gap. Everyone thinks they're reliable; friends know the truth.

**Q8 — Being dependable in a crisis**
- **Self:** "When someone in my life needs help in a pinch, I'm one of the first people they can count on."
- **Friend:** "When you need help in a pinch, [Name] is one of the first people you can count on."
- **Expected gap:** Mixed. Some underrate their dependability (hidden strength). Others overrate it (blind spot).

---

### Category 5: Composure

**Q9 — Calm under pressure**
- **Self:** "I come across as calm and steady, even when I'm stressed or overwhelmed inside."
- **Friend:** "[Name] comes across as calm and steady, even in stressful situations."
- **Expected gap:** Hidden strength. Most people think their stress is more visible than it is.

**Q10 — Handling setbacks**
- **Self:** "I handle unexpected changes or setbacks without getting thrown off."
- **Friend:** "[Name] handles unexpected changes or setbacks without getting thrown off."
- **Expected gap:** Hidden strength. People underestimate how composed they appear to others.

---

### Category 6: Confidence

**Q11 — Making people comfortable**
- **Self:** "I make people feel comfortable and welcome, especially in new or awkward situations."
- **Friend:** "[Name] makes people feel comfortable and welcome, especially in new or awkward situations."
- **Expected gap:** Hidden strength. Most people underrate this, especially introverts.

**Q12 — Making an impression**
- **Self:** "I leave a strong impression on people when they first meet me."
- **Friend:** "[Name] left a strong impression on you when you first met them."
- **Expected gap:** Hidden strength. The "liking gap" research shows we chronically underestimate how memorable we are.
